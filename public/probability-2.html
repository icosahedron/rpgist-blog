So one die is easy enough. How about 2 dice, or more? Elementary, I'm sure.

2 Die For

Dice is actually the plural, but it's not as catchy.

2 or more dice are not much different than a single die, but requires a little addition, and usually something to track combinations of the dice. Here's a sample for a d4:

You probably have noticed that some totals appear more often than others. 2 and 8 only appear once each, while 5 appears 4 times. There are a total of 16 combinations.

How we determine the chances of a certain number showing on a single die? Take the number of times that number appears on the die and divide it by the number of faces on the die.

And that's it. That's the basics of probability for a single roll, no matter how many dice are rolled.

Secret Combinations

How many combinations are there? You just multiply the number of sides of each dice by the number of dice. So 2d4 = 4 * 4 = 16. What about 3d8? 8*8*8 = 256.

And what about 2 different die combination, such as d4 and d10? 4 * 10 = 40 different combinations.

So how often would a 12 come up on d4+d10? Okay, so what combination of a d4 and a d10 total to 12?
