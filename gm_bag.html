GM Bag (GenCon Edition)

A while ago on the podcast I participate in, RPG Circus, I did an segment on my GM Bag. While I felt it was a good exposition, I thought I could follow up here with more detail on the bag itself and include some images of its contents.

Also, since GenCon is fast approaching, I will cover what I will be bringing to the convention.

The Bag

What kind of bag does a discerning GM carry?

The obvious candidate is Think Geek’s Bag of Holding. One of the players in my group has this bag, and it’s a high quality bag. You really can’t go wrong with it.

But my tastes are more simple. I have the Rapiddominance Classic Military Messenger Bag. It is basically the same dimensions and about half the price. Also, I prefer the beige color.

GM Bag

Pockets Galore

The flap of this bag reaches over to cover the entire bag. It velcros closed.

The bag has a large central compartment that can fit a 17" laptop and several books, which we’ll see below. It closes with a zipper, which keeps loose objects from spilling out if you pick the bag up in a hurry.

Inside the main compartment is a small, zippered, papers pocket. It’s here that I store my GenCon event tickets and even my wallet when I don’t want to carry it in my pocket. Short of stealing the entire bag, it would be difficult for someone to pull something out of this compartment.

On the front are two large pockets, also zippered. They can hold quite a few things. The flap reaches over these pockets and adheres to the velcro strips on the front of these pockets.

There are two side pouches. These don’t zipper, but may snap shut.

Last, the back holds a document pouch that velcros shut. This is a convenient pouch for holding quick items that you might need access to, but I wouldn’t hold important items here. They would be at risk for falling out or being snatched too easily.

Books

The bag itself is important, but you can’t play with an empty bag. It needs game books.
